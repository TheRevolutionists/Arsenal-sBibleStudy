James 1:13-20 - Dealing with Temptation

For Next Time

Try to spend more time on the scripture itself.

Key Word Study

The word "err" - To go astray, to wander.

Scripture Passages & Notes

James 1:13 (KJV)

"Let no man say when he is tempted, I am tempted of God: for God cannot be tempted with evil, neither tempteth he any man:"

The Lord allows us to be tempted to test our faith. It's easy to do good when everything is going good. Our faith is truly tested when we are going through hard times. When you're facing negativity. As we are tempted we can use that to grow spiritually and deny those temptations and those thoughts.

We all make mistakes but it's most important to remember God loves us and to turn to the Bible.

James 1:14 (KJV)

"But every man is tempted, when he is drawn away of his own lust, and enticed."

James 1:15 (KJV)

"Then when lust hath conceived, it bringeth forth sin: and sin, when it is finished, bringeth forth death."

If you give in to what you want, it won't end in a good way.

James 1:19 (KJV)

"Wherefore, my beloved brethren, let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath:"

James 1:21 (KJV)

"Wherefore lay apart all filthiness and superfluity of naughtiness, and receive with meekness the engrafted word, which is able to save your souls."

Surrender to the Lord. Allow his word to enter your soul.

James 1:22 (KJV)

"But be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving your own selves."

Cross References

Scripture

James 1:13-15 addresses the source of temptation, making it clear that God doesn't tempt us with evil. Instead, temptation comes from our own desires that lead us astray.

Observation

James provides a progression of how sin develops: desire leads to temptation, temptation leads to sin, and sin leads to death. He emphasizes personal responsibility in the process of temptation.

Application

We need to be aware of our own desires and recognize when they might lead us astray. When facing temptation, we should remember that God provides strength to overcome it and use these moments as opportunities for spiritual growth.

Prayer

Lord, help us recognize the source of our temptations and give us strength to resist them. May we grow spiritually through trials rather than falling into sin. Remind us that you love us even when we make mistakes, and guide us back to your Word.

Scripture

James 1:19-22 gives practical guidance on how to live as a believer, emphasizing listening, controlling speech and anger, and being doers of the Word.

Observation

These verses provide practical guidance for Christian living. James emphasizes the importance of not just hearing God's word but putting it into action in our daily lives.

Application

We should practice being quick to listen and slow to speak, especially when angry. Our faith shouldn't just be intellectual—we need to actively apply Scripture's teachings in our daily decisions and interactions.

Prayer

Father, help us to surrender to you completely and allow your Word to transform us from within. Give us the wisdom to be doers of your Word, not just hearers. Help us listen more than we speak and control our anger when faced with challenges.
